My Motivation:

My wife is an educator. In their academia world they focus a lot on filling in the “gaps.” For lack of a better term, I too have found my personality leaning in this gap-filling role direction. In school, gaps refer to those empty crevacies that were over-looked or never quite structurally sound in the mind and curriculum being taught to children. In my life I like to see gaps as opportunities. I enjoy the thrill of pursuing the problem solving process. It usually begins with a person, sometimes frustrated and aware and other times simply wishing/needing something they cannot make happen. From the dialogue during an initial needs-assesment to the construction and implementation of a plan, I love gap-filling. But from my paper resume - you’d never know this was the role I routinely filled in the organizations I have served. In addition to filling this role for my co-workers and volunteers, I’ve been fueled by this opportunity as a mentor to several and have even been a part of two small-buisness start-ups as a result of my passion for the gaps. There is a sense of pride in filling a need that motivates me to be a top performer.
